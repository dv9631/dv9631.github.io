      5.3 Failure mode, effects, and criticality analysis (FMECA)

      5.3.1 purpose of analysis

      1. qualitative measures of magnitude of failure mode effect
      2. impact or importance of a failure mode
      3. quantify the relative magnitude of each failure rate
      4. aid to decision making
      5. combination of criticality and severity,priority for action to mitigate or minimize effect of certain failures

      5.3.2 risk, R and risk priority number

      1. subjective measure of the severity of the effect and an estimate of the expected probability of its occurance for a predetermined time period assumed for analysis
      2. RPN=SxP | where : S= severity, an estimate oh how strongly the effects of the failure will affect the system or user
        |p = probability of occurence, lkelihood that the failure effect will occur
      3. RPN= SxOxD | where: S= severity as described above | O: probability of occurence |D: detectability, identify and eliminate the failure before the system or customer is affected
      4. RPN used to prioritise in addressing the mitigation of failure modes
      5. if similar or identical rpn, high severity numbers are addressed first
      6. high priority is assigned to high rpn
      7. rpn exceeding a defined threshold are not acceptable
      8. high importance is given to the high severity numbers

      5.3.3 Relationship between FMECA and risk analysis

      • FMECA usually results in a relative ranking of the contribution to the overall risk
      • risk analysis for high risk systems generally aims at risk acceptability
      • high risk effects is recognised it is adviced that a probabilistic risk analysis(PRA) should be used in preference to FMECA
      • probabilistic risk analysis, where also more influential parameters (and their interactions) can be taken into account , eg:exposure time,probability of avoidancej,latency failures , fault detecttion mechanisms
